vIn a strict mathematical sense, a mirror doesn't reverse left and right; it reverses front and back. When you look North, your reflection looks South. For asymmetrical objects, this mirror reversal automatically transforms them into their enantiomorph.

A familiar example of enantiomorphs is your own hands, one is the mirror image of the other. They appear identical, yet they are opposites; to transform one into the other, you would have to flip it through a higher dimension.
